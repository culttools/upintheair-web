Starting long distance, then getting married

We met in a lounge. Six months of overlapping itineraries later, we stopped overlapping and started choosing.

This is a placeholder story. When we have permission, we’ll run the real one.

They met in a lounge. Both had added the same airport, same three-hour window. The app said “on the way.” They matched. She was in the corner with a laptop; he was one table over. They started talking. He had a flight to London; she had one to Dublin. They’d both be back through the same hub in two weeks.

So they did it again. And again. For six months, their relationship was a series of overlaps: same airport, same lounge, same “we have two hours.” They didn’t live in the same city. They didn’t plan to. But the overlaps added up. Eventually one of them said: what if we stopped relying on the schedule and started choosing the same place?

From overlap to choice

That’s the transition we care about. The app doesn’t promise “the one.” It promises “someone on your way.” Sometimes that’s a coffee. Sometimes it’s a marriage. We’re not in charge of the outcome. We’re in charge of the overlap. The rest is up to you.
