Dating when nothing’s guaranteed

Behavioral cues in situations where you might never see each other again.

Most dating happens in a context of “we could do this again.” Same city, same social graph, same apps. But when you meet someone on a layover or a train, there’s no guarantee you’ll ever be in the same place at the same time again. That changes behavior.

People are more honest when the stakes feel finite. They’re more likely to say what they actually want—a coffee, a conversation, a “we’ll see”—because there’s no performative “second date” script. They’re also more likely to show up. When the window is 47 minutes, you don’t flake. You show up or you don’t.

Low commitment, high clarity

The app is built for “we might never meet again” as a feature, not a bug. That forces a different kind of cue: “I’m here, you’re here, do we want to spend this slice of time together?” No long-term promise. No profile optimization for the algorithm. Just overlap and choice.

What we’re learning

We’re watching how people behave when the default isn’t “we’ll match and maybe text forever.” It’s “we have an hour in Denver.” The behavioral cues are different. So are the outcomes. Some people want that. We’re building for them.
