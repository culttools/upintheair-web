Is romance dead after the apps?

Or just relocated to gate B12 and the 6:42 to Paddington?

Everyone says the apps killed romance. Endless scrolling, ghosting, the sense that someone better is one swipe away. But maybe romance didn’t die—it just moved. It moved to the places where we’re not “shopping”: the airport, the train, the layover, the line. Places where the stakes feel different because the context is shared.

When you match with someone “on the way,” you’re not matching in a void. You’re matching in a slice of time and place you both chose (or ended up in). That’s not the same as “we’re both in this city.” It’s “we’re both in this city right now, with a limited window, and we could have a coffee or we could never see each other again.” The constraint is the point.

Gate B12

We’ve heard from people who met at gate B12. Not “we matched and then met at a bar.” We matched because we were both at gate B12. The app didn’t create the romance; it revealed the overlap. The romance was already possible. The app just made it findable.

The 6:42 to Paddington

Same idea on the train. You’ve been taking the same train for years. So has someone else. You never looked up. The app doesn’t “create” the meeting; it surfaces that you’re on the same track. What you do with that is still up to you. That’s romance—not dead, just waiting at the platform.
