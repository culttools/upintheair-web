Stories from the way

Real and speculative. People who met at airports, in lounges, after cancellations—and some who travelled the same route for years and never crossed paths.

Airport · Layover

The 47-minute layover

I had a tight connection in Minneapolis. She was waiting for the same onward flight, three gates down. The app said we had 47 minutes of overlap. I sent a like; she’d already liked me. We met at the only open coffee place in the concourse.

“I still think about that cinnamon roll. We didn’t exchange numbers until we were both on the plane. Now we plan layovers on purpose.”

Train · Cancellation

Cancelled train, same platform

Everything was delayed. I was standing on the platform with a hundred other people, and the app said someone else on my “trip” was there too. We started complaining to each other. Two hours later we were in a pub. The train never came. We didn’t care.

“We’d both been trying to get to the same city. We got there the next day. Together.”

Lounge

The lounge at 5am

Neither of us could sleep. The app showed we had a three-hour overlap at the same airport before our respective early flights. We didn’t match until we were already there. She was in the lounge. I walked over. We talked until boarding.

“It felt like a date that had been scheduled by chaos. In a good way.”

Line

Security line, same terminal

We’d both put in that we’d be at the same airport around the same window. The app said “on the way” and “short overlap.” We matched. I said I was in the security line. She said she was too. We met at the gate on the other side. Thirty seconds of “hi” before her flight. We texted for a month. Then we met again on purpose.

Speculative

The same route for 12 years

We’d both been taking the 7:14 to the city. Same line, same direction. Twelve years. We’d never spoken. The app matched us because we’d both added that train as a recurring trip. We had no idea we’d been sharing the same carriage.

“The first time we met on purpose, we couldn’t stop laughing. We’d been right there the whole time.”

Speculative

Never met

Sometimes we wonder about the people we didn’t match with. The ones on the same flight, the same train, the same layover—who we passed in the corridor or sat two rows behind. The app only shows you who’s on the way; it doesn’t show you everyone. So there are still people out there who travelled the same routes for years and never knew. Maybe that’s okay. Maybe the right ones show up when you’re ready to look.
