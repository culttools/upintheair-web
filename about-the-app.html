About the app

How it works—privacy, safety, and technical.

Exactly how it works

You add your travel: flights and trains, with places (airport or station codes) and time windows. We don’t use GPS or live location. We only use your itinerary.

Our system finds other users whose itineraries overlap with yours—same place (e.g. Denver, Gare du Nord) and overlapping time. We don’t show “same flight” or “same train” in the feed. We show “on the way.” That’s deliberate: you stay in control of what you reveal. If you match, you see a time and place where you could meet (e.g. “DEN, 2–5pm”). Where you actually meet—coffee, lounge, gate—is up to you.

You swipe like or pass. If you both like, it’s a match. You then see the overlap window. No obligation to meet; no live tracking.

Privacy

We never show your exact flight, train, or seat. Discovery is itinerary-based only: place + time window. We don’t sell or share your travel data. We don’t use your location after you’ve entered a trip. You can add trips manually, by forwarding confirmation emails, or by connecting your calendar—we treat that data as sensitive and access-controlled.

Safety

“On the way” instead of “same flight” reduces the chance someone uses the app to target you on a specific plane or train. You choose whether to reveal more after matching. We support reporting and moderation. Meeting in a public place (airport, station, lounge) is built into the product—you’re never asked to share a home address.

Technical

The app is built to run on the web and as a native-style experience. We use LynxJS where it fits: one codebase for iOS, Android, and web, with a React-like model so we can ship quickly and keep the experience consistent. The current proof-of-concept is a PWA (install from the browser); the production app can be delivered via Lynx for native stores while keeping the same logic and design. The backend stores only what’s needed for matching and auth—no surplus tracking.
